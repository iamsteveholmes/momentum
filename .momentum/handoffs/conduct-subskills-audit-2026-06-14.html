Source-verified, multi-lens audit of the /momentum:conduct dependency graph

Executive summary

The conduct sub-skill graph is architecturally sound — the verification pass confirmed sole git-mutation authority, consistent authority invariants, a shallow well-enforced spawn graph, present reference contracts, and (correcting the earlier single-pass audit) working agent resolution and complete rehydration routing. This is a tune-up, not a teardown. But three real clusters surfaced:

  1. One genuine contract bug (critical). The Conductor invokes the directed fixer at step 2.S3 (workflow.md:956) with findings that were never assigned a finding_id — the normalization at :700–727 omits it, yet the fixer contract requires the Conductor to assign it first. This is a true violation, missed by the first audit.
  2. Resume / ledger idempotency gaps (high). The five finding-disposition append sites (:985–1017) and the stage3-escalation append (:1026) skip the {{ledger_seen_events}} duplicate-prevention check mandated at :404; and the rehydration block (:260–394) doesn't restate the REHYDRATION EXEMPTION (:217) inline. Together these risk duplicate findings and ledger doubling on every resume.
  3. Reference-path hygiene (high). stakes-classification-rubric.md is referenced under conductor/references/ but lives in skills/momentum/references/; shared contracts are cited with mixed bare vs. full paths; the AVFL merge-review output shape is consumed without a cross-reference.

The remainder are low-severity convention items (mixed model-ID styling, a stray claude-opus-4-7 in triage, over-long descriptions, missing sub-step numbering). Net: fix the finding_id bug and the idempotency cluster; the rest is polish.
