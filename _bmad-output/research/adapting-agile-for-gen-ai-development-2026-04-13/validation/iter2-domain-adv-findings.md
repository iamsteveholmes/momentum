**Skepticism:** Balanced (Level 2)
**Stage:** Re-validation after iteration 1 fixes

## Summary

Total findings: 9
- critical: 2
- high: 4
- medium: 3
- low: 0

By dimension:
- domain_rule_compliance: 2
- convention_adherence: 2
- fitness_for_purpose: 5

The iteration 1 fixes addressed verifiable factual errors (Kinetic Enterprise attribution, AI-DLC three-phase structure in main body, METR direction-of-effect, Anthropic Bloom unverifiability, ATDD/BDD disambiguation, Forrester provenance). Those fixes are real and meaningful — the corpus is no longer misattributing concepts to authors who didn't write them.

But the corpus's three core fitness-for-purpose problems remain. After iteration 1, a practitioner reading this corpus to answer the scope's three explicit questions still gets:

1. **Story granularity solution** — Still a vocabulary list (Bolts, Units of Work, Agent Stories, Agentic Developer Stories, Impact Loops, Super-Specs, Context Capsules) with no operational sizing rule. The "synthesis: a pragmatic model for 2026" in research-work-granularity-ai-speed.md prescribes a six-element stack but does not give a sizing rule for any element. DOMAIN-002 (iter 1) is unaddressed.

2. **Specification-completeness solution** — The five-layer "defense in depth" remains structurally circular: humans are the value-validation gate (Layer 4), but the cognitive-load file documents that humans cannot serve as effective gates at AI velocity. DOMAIN-007 (iter 1) is unaddressed.

3. **Behavioral validation against running apps** — The Blind Tester pattern correctly carries a known-gap note (good fix), but the corpus's central recommended stack (OpenSpec + DeepEval/MLflow) is still unverified. The maintainer of OpenSpec ("Fission-AI") cannot be confirmed; DeepEval and agentevals descriptions remain uncorroborated. DOMAIN-009 (iter 1) is materially unaddressed.

Additionally, the AI-DLC fix is incomplete and now internally contradictory: the main body says three-phase Inception/Construction/Operations, but Q1 still says "AI-DLC officially abandons the sprint in favor of 'Bolts'" and "Units of Work." A reader cannot tell from the corpus whether AI-DLC is phase-based, bolt-based, or both. The fix introduced a new coherence problem.

The non-Agile alternatives (Shape Up, Zero-Backlog, Kinetic Enterprise) remain at paragraph depth despite the scope explicitly stating openness to abandoning Agile. The Kinetic Enterprise [UNVERIFIED] note is the right move but does not add the missing depth.

TDA is still presented as a methodology evolution of TDD with no described mechanical difference from canonical Red-Green-Refactor. The fix log does not record any change here. DOMAIN-004 (iter 1) is unaddressed.

What practitioners would still be left without: an operational sizing rule for AI-era work units, a verified open-source harness recipe they can stand up Monday morning, a mechanism that actually breaks the human-as-bottleneck circularity, and a clear answer to "what does AI-DLC actually look like in practice?"
