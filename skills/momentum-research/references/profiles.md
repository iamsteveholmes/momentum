# Research Profiles

Three profiles control the depth, cost, and rigor of a research session.

| Aspect | Light | Medium | Heavy |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| **Subagents** | 3 | 5-6 | 6-8 |
| **Sub-questions** | 3 | 5-6 | 6-8 |
| **AVFL** | Skip | checkpoint, corpus, 2 lenses (structural + accuracy) | full, corpus, 4 lenses |
| **Q&A** | Skip | Yes — AVFL-targeted questions | Yes — AVFL-targeted questions |
| **Gemini** | Optional | Optional | Optional |
| **Estimated tokens** | ~100K | ~300K | ~600K |
| **Estimated time** | ~15 min | ~45 min | ~2-3 hours |
| **Rate limit risk** | Low | Moderate — stagger launches | High — risk of Sustain Limit |

## When to Use Each

**Light:** Quick research for low-stakes decisions. "What are the options for X?" Produces a summary with sources but no verification layer.

**Medium:** Substantive research informing real decisions. New technology evaluation, architecture research, competitive analysis. Includes cross-document verification and practitioner input.

**Heavy:** High-stakes research. Publishing externally, informing major architectural decisions, comprehensive domain analysis. Full adversarial validation with dual reviewers.

## Profile Selection Guidance

Default: **medium**. Most research benefits from at least a verification pass. Use light only when speed matters more than rigor. Use heavy only when the research output will be consumed by others or will inform irreversible decisions.
