## Story

As a developer reviewing AI-produced output at every human gate,
I want a practice-wide decision-grade presentation standard with measurable caps and a self-sufficiency floor,
so that my cognitive budget is spent on decision-relevant content — tight on the irrelevant, complete on the decision-relevant.

## Description

DEC-036 D5 / Phase 4 (explicitly separable from the conduct build; can proceed in parallel). Establish a practice-wide standard for human-facing output: measurable caps (≤N bullets / word budgets), executive-summary-first, positive-concision phrasing, output schemas — paired with a hard self-sufficiency floor: every decision carries its what / why / evidence INLINE, never sending the human to reference other material. The convention: `effort` drives work depth; explicit caps govern output verbosity. Applies to the conduct report AND the live conversational skills (assessment, decision, sprint-dev/conduct, retro, impetus).

**Pain context:** "Specification Fatigue" — spending cognitive budget on irrelevant parts. AES-004 Finding 6 flagged that conduct's self-sufficiency mandate is good but lacks a concision counterweight. The caps-half currently has NO owner (`self-sufficiency-validator` enforces only the floor). This needs its own practice-wide intake — a decision-grade-presentation rule plus per-skill cap wiring. Source: DEC-036 D5 / Phase 4; connects to prior `spec-fatigue-research-2026-03-21`.
